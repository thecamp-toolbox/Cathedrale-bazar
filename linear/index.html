À quoi ressemble le bazar en pratique ?

1. Créer du désir avec une mission claire et ambitieuse

Imagine a world in which every single person on the planet is given free access to the sum of all human knowledge. That's what we're doing

Jimmy Wales, Wikipedia

Simon Sinek

2. Rendre l'organisation poreuse

Sharing among an entire an entire ecosystem of innovative partners is now a crucial step to modernization

Brian Solis

#community #crowdsourcing

3. Encourager les initiatives & minimaliser les friction

We’ve heard that other companies have people allocate a percentage of their time to self-directed projects. At Valve, that percentage is 100.

Valve Employee Handbook

Les 4 étapes de l'engagement

  • 01. Observation

    Montrer / comprendre

  • 02. Participation

    Apprendre / faire

  • 03. Contribution

    Faire avec / partager

  • 04. Activation

    Faire faire / permettre

4. Tourner la gouvernance sur ceux qui font

Squads have end to end responsability for the stuff they build.

Spotify "training & development" blog

#doocracy #governance

5. Écrivez vos propres règles

An Organization’s Culture is Its Personality. It is the company’s shared beliefs, values, and practices — the unique way your organization sees the world and acts. It is what employees do and what their actions mean to them.

  2. Immédiateté
  3. Participation

  5. Autonomie

6. Rendre tout transparent

When Joel first introduced Buffer’s transparent salaries, it was both frightening and exciting.

Buffer Open blog

#transparence #communs

https://github.com/twbs/bootstrap/issues

7. Préférer les systèmes plutôt que les produits

You [can] think of hardware as a piece of culture you want to share with other people.

Massimo Banzi, Fondateur, Arduino

#system #opensource

8. Permettre des modifications

Given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow

Eric Raymond
